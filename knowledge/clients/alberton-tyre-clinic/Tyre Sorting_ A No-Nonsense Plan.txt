Here is your zero-BS guide to reading a tyre so you know what you are looking at, followed by the exact battle plan to execute right this minute.

1. The 10-Second Tyre Reading Cheat Sheet

Look at the side of the tyre. You are looking for a sequence of numbers molded into the rubber that looks something like 265/65 R17.

Here is exactly what that means and how much you should care about it for sorting:

The Number

What it Means

Your Action

265

Width: How wide the tyre is in millimeters.

Second sorting priority.

65

Profile: The thickness of the sidewall (percentage of the width).

Third sorting priority.

R

Radial: The construction type.

Ignore it entirely. Means nothing for stacking.

17

Rim Size: The diameter of the wheel in inches.

PRIMARY SORTING NUMBER. This dictates exactly which shelf the tyre goes to.

Brand

e.g., Goodyear, Dunlop, Firestone.

Final sorting priority, after the sizes are grouped together.

Rule of thumb: When you pick up a tyre, your eyes should immediately dart to that last number (the rim size). That tells you its neighborhood. The width and profile tell you its exact house.

2. The "Right Now" Execution Plan

Step 1: Clear a Staging Area (The "Drop Zone")

You need empty floor space to sort. Shove a bunch of the chaos to one side to create a clear, open 3x3 meter space. This is where you'll drag tyres to figure out what they are before racking them.

Step 2: Excommunicate the Trash & Abandoned Stock

Do not let junk clog up your prime shelving or your brain space right now.

Grab all the second-hand tyres and drag them to the worst, darkest corner of the room.

Grab all the "customer left their mags/tyres here" stock and stack it in a completely separate pile out of the way. Deal with them later.

Step 3: The "Big Sort" (By Rim Size Only)

Do not worry about the brand, the width, or the profile right now. That will slow you down. Just look at the rim size (the 14, 15, 16, 17, etc.) and make rough piles on the floor.

Pile 1: All 13" and 14"

Pile 2: All 15"

Pile 3: All 16"

Pile 4: All 17" (Expect a lot of these for bakkies and SUVs)

Pile 5: 18" and up (The big boys)

Step 4: The "Micro Sort" & Racking

Now that you have piles of identical rim sizes, tackle them one by one. Let's say you start with the 15" pile.

Group them by Width and Profile (e.g., put all the 195/65 R15s together).

Now you look at the brand. If you have ten 195/65 R15s, and six are Firestone and four are Continental, put the six Firestones together and the four Continentals right next to them.

Load them onto the shelf vertically (standing up), with the tread stickers facing out into the aisle.

Step 5: Prime Real Estate (Closest to the Door)

As you rack, put the most common, high-turnover sizes right by the door. In Gauteng, this usually means common passenger sizes (like 14" and 15") and standard bakkie sizes (like 245/70 R16 or 265/65 R17). Put the weird, low-profile sports car tyres and massive off-road mud-terrains at the very back of the storeroom.

Get in there and start dragging those abandoned mags and second-hand tyres to a corner first—that immediately frees up physical space and builds momentum.
