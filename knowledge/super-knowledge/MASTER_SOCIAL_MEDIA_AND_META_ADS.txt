SECTION 1 — ECOSYSTEM MAP
================================================================================

Platforms in scope
- Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, WhatsApp (ads and organic touchpoints), Audience
  Network placements where enabled.

Core tools (typical division of labour)
- Meta Business Suite: organic publishing (Planner), unified inbox (comments / DMs
  / WhatsApp where connected), light boosting, Lead Center (lightweight CRM),
  high-level Insights, commerce/shops integration.
- Meta Ads Manager: full paid structure — campaigns, ad sets, ads, budgets,
  detailed targeting (where available), experiments, granular reporting.
- Business Manager (backend): assets, domains, billing, partner access, Pixel /
  dataset and Conversions API setup — governance layer.

Strategic idea
- Organic social feeds the algorithm signals (what people respond to). Paid
  social amplifies and tests. Lead ads and messaging ads close loops without a
  site visit. Treat them as one system, not silos.

================================================================================
SECTION 2 — HOW META AD DELIVERY WORKS (CONCEPTUAL)
================================================================================

Total Value auction (simplified)
- Inventory is auctioned per impression. Winning is not “highest bid only.”
  Meta optimizes for Total Value to the ecosystem, combining roughly:
  - Advertiser bid (what you are willing to pay / automated bid strategy).
  - Estimated action rate (EAR): predicted chance the user takes your desired
    action after seeing the ad (click, lead, purchase, etc.).
  - User value / quality: post-click experience, hide rates, engagement bait,
    misleading creative, slow landings — all can tax delivery.

Creative-as-targeting (modern default)
- The system infers who should see the ad from creative semantics (what the
  image/video shows, what the copy claims) plus behavioral data. Hyper-narrow
  interest stacks can sometimes limit learning; broad geo + age + gender with
  strong creative is a common winning pattern when conversion volume exists.

Entity clustering (practical implication)
- Many near-duplicate creatives (tiny text swaps, same layout, same hook) may
  be treated as one “concept” for exploration. Meaningful tests need meaningful
  visual and narrative divergence — not 20 shades of the same idea.

GEM / sequencing (conceptual)
- The system may learn which format or sequence works for different users
  (e.g., short video then static retarget). Feed it a diverse asset library.

Signal engineering mindset
- Your job is to supply: clear offers, fast destinations, honest creative,
  conversion signal (Pixel / Conversions API / offline conversions / CRM uploads),
  and creative breadth — so the ML can learn.

================================================================================
SECTION 3 — ACCOUNT STRUCTURE PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

Classic hierarchy
- Campaign: objective / budget type (often CBO — campaign budget optimization).
- Ad set: audience, placements, optimization event, schedule, sometimes bid cap.
- Ad: creative + copy + destination.

Consolidation vs fragmentation
- Many tiny ad sets starve learning. Prefer fewer, broader ad sets when budgets
  are small, unless a segment truly needs isolation (different offer, geo, or
  margin).

Practical patterns
- Testing lane: prospecting broad + new angles (creative diversity).
- Scaling lane: proven winners, sometimes Advantage+ catalog or consolidated
  shopping-style setups for e-commerce.
- Retargeting lane: site visitors, engagers, lead lists — usually smaller
  budgets, different message.

“Four peaks” calendar thinking (cash flow + signal)
- Plan several major commercial peaks per year (seasonal, cultural, industry).
  Peaks inject conversion volume that retrains the system; valleys need lighter
  spend or brand/content maintenance.

================================================================================
SECTION 4 — OBJECTIVES (CHOOSE TO MATCH THE REAL CONVERSION)
================================================================================

Match the optimization event to business truth
- If you optimize for traffic but need leads, you get cheap clicks, not leads.
- If you optimize for purchases without purchase volume, the system cannot learn.

Common mappings
- Awareness / reach: new categories, launches, narrative shifts.
- Engagement: social proof building, community growth (watch cost per meaningful
  engagement, not vanity).
- Traffic: only when the landing experience is excellent or you need mid-funnel
  education clicks.
- Leads: lead forms or messaging destinations — great when speed matters.
- Sales / catalog: e-commerce with catalog and purchase events firing reliably.

Messaging / WhatsApp / Instagram DMs
- Conversion location set to messaging when conversation is the product (high
  consideration, appointment booking, custom quotes).
- Pre-filled prompts + instant auto-reply + qualification quick replies reduce
  drop-off. Human handoff for complex sales.

SECTION 5 — CREATIVE FORMATS: SPECS, RATIOS, SAFE ZONES
================================================================================

Image (feed / general)
- Common ratios: 1:1 square, 4:5 vertical (often strong mobile feed presence),
  1.91:1 landscape (some placements).
- Practical minimum: 1080 px on the short edge for many placements; avoid soft
  or over-compressed JPEGs.
- Copy guidance (platform truncates): primary text ~125 chars visible before
  “See more”; headline ~40 chars; description short (often ~30 chars).

Video / Reels / Stories
- Vertical 9:16 (e.g., 1080×1920) for Reels/Stories-first creative.
- Feed video often 4:5 or 1:1; long runtimes allowed technically, but short ads
  frequently outperform for DR.
- Sound-off design: captions, readable supers, visual story without audio.
- Thumb-stop: first 1–3 seconds decide retention; platform uses early retention
  as a quality signal.

Safe zones (critical)
- Reels: dense UI (caption, buttons, side stack). Keep key text/logo OUT of the
  bottom ~35% and respect top/right margins — “UI collision” kills readability
  and performance.
- Stories: similar 9:16 canvas but typically less bottom obstruction than Reels;
  still keep bottom ~20% cautious for CTA/swipe UI.
- Universal discipline: “center safe” — place hero message and product in a
  central box (roughly square region in a 9:16 canvas) so one master asset can
  survive multiple placements.

Carousel
- Cards: minimum 2, maximum 10 (platform limits).
- Cards should share ONE aspect ratio within the ad (commonly 1:1; some teams
  prefer 4:5 vertical cards for feed dominance — check current ad-level support
  in Ads Manager).
- Headlines per card short; use carousel for story steps, comparisons, bundles,
  or seamless panoramic slices.

Collection / Instant Experience
- Mobile-first catalog storytelling; hero + product grid; opens fast native
  experience — strong when mobile site is weak (still eventually convert on site
  or messaging).

Lead ads
- Instant forms reduce friction; add qualifying questions for quality. CRM sync
  or webhook automation matters — speed-to-lead wins.

================================================================================
SECTION 6 — COPY AND CREATIVE PSYCHOLOGY (PLATFORM-NATIVE)
================================================================================

Hooks
- Pattern interrupt: break the visual monotony of the feed (unexpected stillness
  can work in a sea of noise, or bold contrast / motion).
- Zeigarnik / open loops: carousel card 1 starts a thought finished on swipe.
- Curiosity gap: promise specific knowledge (“the hidden reason X fails”) with
  ethical delivery.

Frameworks
- AIDA on carousel progression: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action.
- PAS for services: Problem → Agitate → Solution.
- Mini-headlines on each slide: 4–7 words, skimmable.

Social proof & specificity
- Numbers, timelines, guarantees (where legal), certifications, reviews,
  before/after (honest, compliant).

Creative diversity checklist (for real tests)
- Change at least two of: format (static vs UGC vs motion type), persona shown,
  environment/scene, primary benefit angle (save time vs status vs safety).

Budget split idea (portfolio)
- ~60% proven control creatives, ~30% structured iterations on winners, ~10%
  wildcards — protects revenue while hunting new Entity-level concepts.

================================================================================
SECTION 7 — TARGETING AND AUDIENCES (GENERIC PLAYBOOK)
================================================================================

Broad vs narrow
- Start broad when learning is the goal and creative encodes the audience (“for
  fleet managers hauling max load” calls in the right people).
- Narrow when legal/compliance, geography, or offer truly requires it.

Custom audiences
- Site visitors (Pixel), engagers, video viewers, customer lists, offline
  purchasers — use for retargeting and exclusions (“already bought”).

Lookalikes
- Work best with clean seed lists (high-LTV purchasers, not mixed junk).

Exclusions (often overlooked)
- Exclude impossible customers (wrong geography, incompatible housing for bulk
  delivery, job seekers clicking ads, etc.) when your data proves waste.

Location strategy
- Radius around store / service area; layered saved locations for testing;
  compare performance by zone and reallocate budget.

================================================================================
SECTION 8 — MEASUREMENT, PIXEL, AND DATA HYGIENE
================================================================================

Pixel / dataset
- Install base code; fire standard events (Lead, Purchase, ViewContent, etc.)
  accurately; dedupe with Conversions API where possible.

Event match quality
- Better customer data parameters → better attribution and optimization.

Reporting
- Ads Manager for paid diagnostics (CPM, CTR, CPA, ROAS where applicable).
- Business Suite for organic + holistic trends. Do not compare unrelated KPIs
  across tools without context.

Fatigue signals
- Rising CPM with flat results, declining CTR, rising frequency — refresh
  creative or expand net-new concepts.

SECTION 9 — ORGANIC SOCIAL: PLANNING AND OPERATIONS
================================================================================

Planner / scheduling
- Batch produce; schedule FB + IG with per-platform caption tweaks (tone,
  hashtags on IG, shorter on FB if needed).

Active times
- Use account insights to see when followers are online; post slightly before
  peak windows for early velocity.

Content pillars (rotate weekly)
- Educate (why problems happen), Prove (process, data, guarantees), Humanize
  (team, humor, culture), Convert (offer, urgency, CTA), Community (local
  events, partners).

B2B vs B2C split (when both exist)
- Week start: operational pain / uptime / ROI for commercial buyers.
- Midweek: technical authority and risk reduction.
- Week end: lifestyle, weekend use, family safety, leisure gear.

Local relevance
- Roads, weather, infrastructure, regional holidays, school calendar, local
  markets — these are “free” hooks that increase shareability.

SECTION 10 — HOW TO BUILD A 30-DAY SOCIAL CALENDAR (METHOD)
================================================================================

Step 1 — Define the month’s strategic lens
- Pick ONE primary commercial push (e.g., preventive maintenance before peak
  season, not the obvious season when everyone advertises — invert the narrative
  if science supports it).
- List secondary lenses (B2B lane, premium lane, entry product lane).

Step 2 — Map real-world calendar
- Public holidays, pay cycles, major sports/school breaks, industry events,
  weather patterns, local events.

Step 3 — Week themes
- Week 1: education + myth busting.
- Week 2: proof + identity + topical tie-ins.
- Week 3: lifestyle + niche use cases + partner/community.
- Week 4: offer recap + urgency + social proof sprint.

Step 4 — Day-of-week choreography
- Sunday: longer captions / educational carousels.
- Monday: B2B / operational.
- Tuesday–Wednesday: technical deep dives, diagnostics, “mistakes to avoid.”
- Thursday: anxiety-relief, prep, “before the weekend.”
- Friday: weekend activation (hobbies, travel, toys, sport).
- Saturday: community events, open hours, DIY tips, human stories.

Step 5 — Asset discipline
- For each day define: format (static / reel / carousel), primary ratio(s)
  (produce 1:1 + 9:16 when budget allows), hook line, CTA, landing or DM path.

Step 6 — QA
- Safe zones, legibility on small screens, brand color/contrast, offer accuracy,
  legal/compliance (claims, disclaimers, regulated industries).

SECTION 11 — PAID SOCIAL CREATIVE ANGLES (CROSS-INDUSTRY)
================================================================================

Angle: phased story
- Card or scene 1 problem → 2 mechanism → 3 outcome; works for physical products
  and services.

Angle: proof of quality
- Instrument readouts, certifications, process photos, before/after (honest).

Angle: logistics trust
- Delivery narrative, inspection-before-pay (if true), clear payment methods,
  realistic timelines.

Angle: risk reversal
- Guarantees, warranties, trials, transparent pricing — only if enforceable.

Angle: neighbor / share
- Bulk purchases split across households — when operationally valid.

Angle: dual-use customer
- Same product, weekday vs weekend story (commuter + adventure) for SUVs,
  contractors, family vehicles.

You typically work across:
- Campaigns list: budgets, status, objectives.
- Ad sets: audiences, placements, optimization, schedules.

SECTION 13 — CHECKLISTS
================================================================================

Pre-launch (paid)
- Objective matches business outcome.
- Pixel fires on key pages; test events validated.
- Creative sizes produced; safe zones checked.
- Primary text within truncation strategy; CTA matches destination.
- Exclusions applied (existing customers where appropriate).
- UTMs or tracking params for web destinations (your analytics standard).

Weekly
- Top ads by ROAS/CPA; losers paused.
- Frequency and CPM trends.
- New creative batches entering test lane.
- Inbox / lead follow-up SLA (minutes/hours, not days).

Organic
- 3–5 posts/week minimum for most SMBs (adjust to capacity); consistency beats
  sporadic bursts.
- Reply to DMs/comments quickly — affects trust and signals.

SECTION 16 — RETAIL / MULTI-SKU SOCIAL AD TABLE (PATTERN)
================================================================================
For shops with many SKUs (tyres, parts, product lines), maintain a spreadsheet or
database with columns such as:
- Product / SKU name
- Ad goal (safety, mileage, performance, seasonal)
- Headline hook (short, feed-safe)
- Primary text (benefits + proof + local trust)
- CTA block: quote / visit / WhatsApp / web (consistent formatting)
- Hashtags: mix of brand + category + geo tags (avoid over-stuffing)

Re-use the pattern: hook emoji line → story → benefit bullets → soft CTA →
contact block → hashtags. Keeps production scalable and brand-consistent.

SECTION 17 — AI CONTENT PIPELINE (GENERIC ARCHITECTURE)
================================================================================
When scaling organic posts with LLM + image models:
- Output structured payloads (e.g., JSON) with separate fields for:
  image_prompt_1_1, image_prompt_9_16, overlay_subtext, facebook_caption,
  content_pillar.
- Dual prompts must differ in spatial framing: square = tight centered subject;
  vertical = wider vertical padding and explicit top/bottom negative space so
  overlays and UI safe zones do not cover the product.
- Optional compositing layer: fixed header/footer brand frame + dynamic subtext
  + CTA footer — yields consistent exports without manual design per post.
- Randomize pillars (product showcase, education, myth bust, seasonal) but keep
  brand environment tokens consistent (your real workshop, lighting, colors).

================================================================================
SECTION 18 — HIGH-END STATIC PRODUCT ADS (SQUARE + VERTICAL PAIRS)
================================================================================
Pattern used for premium physical goods:
- Always generate paired 1:1 and 9:16 from the same concept; align typography
  hierarchy (headline, offer container, footer contacts).
- Specify material truth in prompts (bag mesh opacity, product proportions) to
  reduce “AI drift” and refund risk from misleading imagery.
- Visual fusion styles (examples): “precision product on void background” +
  “cinematic teal/orange grade” — pick ONE hybrid aesthetic per brand and run
  consistently for recognition.

================================================================================
SECTION 19 — FACEBOOK / IG PAGE TRUST LAYER (ORGANIC)
================================================================================
- About: complete categories, hours, service areas, compliance statements where
  relevant (regulated products, safety, warranties).
- Pin posts that answer top objections (delivery, payment, guarantees, returns).
- Albums/Reels highlights: proof, process, team, reviews.
- When scams are common in your category, publish verification content (how to
  identify your official page, order flow, invoice pattern).

================================================================================
SECTION 20 — HOSPITALITY / EXPERIENCE ADS (PATTERN)
================================================================================
- Often two decision paths: primary buyer (e.g., activity-led) and veto-holder
  (comfort, safety, amenities). Creative and retargeting can emphasize different
  benefits while staying honest.
- Seasonality: align paid bursts with booking windows (holidays, weather, local
  school terms in your markets).
- Geo testing: wealthy pockets and travel corridors; measure by zone before
  consolidating budget.
